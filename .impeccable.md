## Design Context

### Users
Affluent 30-50s in Umhlanga/Durban North. Professionals and lifestyle-driven families who spend without overthinking quality experiences. They play padel on weekends, take their kids, have dinner, book the private event for their firm. They know good design — they travel to Cape Town, London, Dubai. They'll notice if something looks cheap.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **Grounded. Alive. Refined.**
Not flash for its own sake. Confident, unhurried. The kind of luxury that doesn't need to announce itself.

### Aesthetic Direction
Soho House / Nobu energy. Dark, moody, intimate, members-club weight. Heavy use of photography. Type as a design object, not just communication. Deep forest green, brass/amber, cream. Lots of negative space. Asymmetric compositions. Nothing centered unless it earns it.

Anti-references: generic sports club websites, SaaS-style layouts, anything that looks like a template.

### Design Principles
1. **Break the rhythm** — never repeat the same section structure twice (label → headline → body → button is the enemy)
2. **Photography carries weight** — images do the talking; text is sparse, selective, intentional
3. **Asymmetry as standard** — left-aligned type, offset compositions, unexpected negative space
4. **Less copy, more confidence** — Soho House doesn't explain itself; neither does SERVE
5. **Dark earns its place** — the dark palette should feel intimate and warm, not cold or corporate
